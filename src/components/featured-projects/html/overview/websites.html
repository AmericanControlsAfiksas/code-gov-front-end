Websites

Most government agencies maintain at least one if not 10s or 100s of websites. Below are some open source government websites that can be reused or serve as inspiration:

United States Web Design System (USWDS)

The United States Web Design System (USWDS) is a library of open source UI components and a visual style guide for U.S. federal government websites. Both the USWDS website as well as USWDS itself are open source.

Known reuses: The USWDS team keeps their own inventory of the 100s of government websites that use the design system

code.gov

code.gov was created as part of the Federal Source Code Policy. The code.gov team evangelizes and follows open source best practices to develop code.gov.

Known reuses: None

VA.gov

VA.gov (previously vets.gov) was developed by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) to better serve veterans and replace the old agency website.

Known reuses: None

login.gov

login.gov is a shared service maintained by the General Services Administration (GSA). It offers the public secure and private online access to participating government programs. With one login.gov account, users can sign in to multiple government agencies.

Known reuses: None

seedfund.nsf.gov

seedfund.nsf.gov is the official website for “America’s Seed Program” managed by the National Science Foundation.

Known reuses: None

18f.gsa.gov

18F.gsa.gov is the official website for 18F, an office of federal employees within the General Services Administration (GSA) that collaborates with other agencies to fix technical problems, build products, and improve how government serves the public through technology.

Known reuses:

  • New York City Planning Labs - Website | GitHub repo

analytics.usa.gov

analytics.usa.gov displays information about usage of federal government websites as a part of the Digital Analytics Program.

Known reuses: analytics.usa.gov has been reused by numerous state and local governments as well as other countries. The DAP team keeps their own inventory of the reuses of analytics.usa.gov. 18F wrote a blog post about tips for reusing the analytics.usa.gov platform based on interviews with 3 local governments that went through the process.

vote.gov

vote.gov helps citizens learn more about how to register to vote in their state or territory.

Known reuses: None

plainlanguage.gov

plainlanguage.gov is an online resource to help federal employees understand and comply with the Plain Writing Act of 2010. It represents a joint effort by the Plain Language Action and Information Network (PLAIN) and the General Services Administration.

Known reuses: None

federalist.18f.gov

federalist.18f.gov is a publishing platform for modern, compliant government websites.

Known reuses: None

openforest.fs.usda.gov

openforest.fs.usda.gov is an online system for purchasing Christmas tree permits.

Known reuses: None
